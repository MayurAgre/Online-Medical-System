Babasaheb Ambedkar Hospital

Dr. Baba Saheb Ambedkar hospital is a prestigious 500 bedded multispecialty hospital with super specialties like Neurosurgery, Nephrology, Pulmonology and Urology. The hospital is located on 29.4 acres of land situated at sec-6 Rohini.It provides comprehensive medical care services to the residents of North and North West Delhi. The foundation stone of the hospital was laid by the then Honâ€™ble Prime minister Shri P.V. Narasimha Rao on 19.11.1991..

The OPD services of the hospital were started on 2.08.1999. Round the clock emergency and maternity services along with 70 bedded indoor facilities were started on 13.8.2001. Presently, hospital is functioning with 500 beds..

Hospital is also running postgraduate DNB courses in Surgery, Ophthalmology, Medicine, Family Medicine, Orthopedics, Paediatrics and Obstetrics & Gynae. Medical college attached to the BSA Hospital is being proposed and going to start from 2016 with 100 undergraduate seats.

Facilities

Intensive Care Units
Multiple ICUs for specific requirements - Surgical ICU, Medical ICU, Liver ICU, Renal ICU, Cardiac ICU, Post-Transplant and others.
CT SCAN - 64 Slices (Dual Energy)
High end CT scanner that provides highest level of accuracy for diagnosing disease conditions of the heart, lung and brain.
X-RAY- Scanogram
A radiographic technique used for showing true dimensions by moving a narrow orthogonal beam of x-rays along the length of the structure being measured, for example, the lower extremities.
Advanced Operation Theatres
12 Modular Operation theatres with state of the art specialist equipment(s) required for performing simple, routine or complex surgeries of the heart, brain, kidney, liver, etc.
3 Tesla MRI Machine
Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) is a test that uses a magnetic field and pulses of radio wave energy to make pictures of organs and structures inside the body. In many cases, MRI gives more in-depth information about structures in the body than can be seen with an X-ray, ultrasound, or computed tomography (CT) scan.
